## FLAGSHIP PROGRAM

**Transform personnel with zero FPV experience into operationally proficient pilots.** This comprehensive program addresses the critical capability gap: units needing rapid FPV deployment without lengthy training pipelines or contractor dependency.

### Program Overview

This intensive training program covers complete aircraft assembly, flight controller configuration, progressive flight training, and tactical mission execution. Based on Army UALC standards and Ukrainian operational lessons learned.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Complete FPV system assembly from components
- Flight controller configuration and tuning
- Intensive simulator and progressive live flight training
- Tactical mission execution and ISR coordination
- Field sustainment and repair capabilities

### Who Should Attend

**Primary Audience:**
- Infantry platoons requiring organic FPV capability
- Special operations elements
- Forward observers and reconnaissance teams
- Combat engineers conducting breach operations
- Military intelligence collection teams

**Prerequisites:** None required (designed for zero prior experience)

### Training Approach

Students build complete FPV systems from components, configure advanced flight controllers, and progress through intensive simulator training before live flight operations. The program emphasizes operator self-sufficiency and field sustainment capabilities—not contractor dependency.

### Program Outcomes

Upon completion, operators demonstrate proficiency in:
- Building complete FPV aircraft from components
- Configuring flight controller systems for tactical operations
- Flying tactical maneuvers in operational scenarios
- Conducting field repairs and sustainment
- Integrating FPV into small unit operations

### Certification

Graduates receive **FPV Tactical Operator Level 1 Certificate** with 24-month validity, including capability ratings across five proficiency domains.

## Program Investment

FFF-401 is priced on a **per-student, cohort-based model.** Pricing varies by customer type (DoD/Government, Commercial, Academic) and cohort size. Optimal cohort is 8 students — custom sizes available from 4 to 12.

**All-inclusive pricing covers:**
- All course materials, manuals, and laminated reference cards
- Complete FPV build kit per student (NDAA-compliant components)
- 3D printing consumables and fabrication materials
- Lead and assistant instructor labor (full 10-day program)
- Facility, equipment, and software licensing
- FPV Tactical Operator Level 1 Certificate upon demonstrated proficiency
- 90-day post-course instructor support

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long is the FFF-401 course?**

FFF-401 is a 10-day program covering 80 contact hours. It runs facility-based in Fayetteville, NC or can be delivered on-site at your location.

---

**Do I need prior FPV experience for FFF-401?**

No prior FPV experience is required. FFF-401 is designed for zero-experience personnel and will bring operators from zero to operationally proficient over the 10-day program.

---

**What equipment is included in FFF-401?**

Every student receives a complete FPV build kit including all components needed to build a tactical FPV aircraft from scratch. Students keep all hardware upon graduation.

---

**How much does FFF-401 cost?**

FFF-401 pricing is structured as a cohort base fee plus a per-student kit fee. Tiered rates are available for DoD/Government, Commercial, and Academic customers. Contact us for a proposal — we respond within 48 hours.

---

**Can FFF-401 be delivered at our location?**

Yes. FFF-401 is available facility-based in Fayetteville, NC or as an on-site deployment to your installation or facility. Contact us to discuss logistics for on-site delivery.

---

**What is the maximum cohort size for FFF-401?**

FFF-401 accommodates a minimum of 4 and maximum of 12 students per cohort. The optimal cohort size is 8 students for equipment management and instructor attention ratios.
