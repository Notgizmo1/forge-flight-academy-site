Additive Manufacturing for UAS Sustainment

Program Overview

This program develops practical additive manufacturing skills for UAS sustainment. Training emphasizes rapid part replacement, not aerospace-grade production. Focus on keeping platforms operational with field-manufactured components.

Key Focus Areas:

  • FDM/FFF 3D printing fundamentals
  • Material selection for UAS applications (LW-PLA, PETG, others)
  • Design for printability and rapid iteration
  • Field printing in austere environments
  • Structural component fabrication
  • Testing and validation of printed parts

Who Should Attend

Primary Audience:

  • Unit sustainment personnel
  • Forward deployed maintenance teams
  • Research and development personnel
  • Training cadre teaching field fabrication
  • Special operations support elements

Prerequisites: Basic UAS operations experience, basic computer literacy

Training Approach

Hands-on printing of actual UAS components. Students design parts, configure printers, print components, and test functionality on actual platforms.

Program Outcomes

Upon completion, personnel demonstrate capability to:

  • Design printable replacement parts from failed components
  • Configure and operate FDM/FFF printers
  • Select appropriate materials for different applications
  • Troubleshoot common printing failures
  • Validate printed parts for operational use
